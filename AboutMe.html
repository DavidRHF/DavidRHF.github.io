About

Hi, I'm David.

I finished a Computer Science B.S. at St. Bonaventure University in May 2026 and I'm heading into a Cybersecurity M.S. next. What pulled me toward security was realizing that the interesting failures happen at the seams — between what a system was designed to do and what it will actually let you do.

My last two semesters were spent on both sides of that. In the fall I was on the systems side: deploying Wazuh agents for host-based intrusion detection, rewriting service configurations in JSON and XML so events came out parseable, and helping build the university's cybersecurity lab from hardware installation through provisioning. In the spring I switched to the red team, running authorized penetration tests with a Hak5 Bash Bunny Mark II and writing keystroke-injection payloads in DuckyScript and Bash.

Doing both in the same year changed how I think about the work. When you've spent a semester tuning a ruleset, you notice how much of your own attack never generated an alert. That gap is the part I want to keep working on.

Before that, four years of coursework in operating systems, databases, networking, and computer architecture, plus a summer at Trinity College, Oxford. I've also worked a dock at Amazon and a register at Hobby Lobby through school — less glamorous, but they taught me how to troubleshoot under time pressure with someone waiting on me.

I'm currently looking for cybersecurity, SOC, and IT roles, and I'm open to relocation. If you're hiring, or you just want to talk shop about physical access attacks or log tuning, send me an email.
